# Schedule Adherence & Intervention Impact Analysis 

## Problem
A student receiving special education services was experiencing significant difficulty adhering to their general education classroom schedule. This resulted in increased staff intervention time, reduced instructional productivity, and strain on classroom resources.

The special education team required objective data to determine whether a schedule modification or additional staffing support was warranted.

## Action
Developed and implemented a structured daily data collection framework to quantify schedule adherence and support needs.

Methodology included:
 - Designing a standardized tracking tool for classroom staff
 - Training team members on consistent data recording protocols
 - Capturing frequency of independent transitions, prompted transitions, and refusal behaviors
 - Aggregating and analyzing one month of observational data
 - Conducting trend analysis to assess trajectory of behavior patterns

## Key Findings
 - The student required additional support in approximately 25% of scheduled transitions
 - Trend analysis indicated escalating difficulty with schedule adherence over time
 - On multiple dates, over 50% of the instructional day was impacted by transition challenges, significantly reducing productive learning time
 - Staff intervention demands were materially higher than typical classroom expectations

## Data-Driven Outcomes
Based on the findings, the team implemented the following evidence-based adjustments:
 - Modified student schedule to incorporate structured breaks and a dedicated one-hour instructional block in the DCD setting
 - Justified and secured approval for an additional one-to-one paraprofessional position using quantitative impact data
 - Reduced disruption to general education classroom instruction
 - Improved alignment between student support needs and staffing allocation

##Tools Used
Excel (data tracking and aggregation), trend analysis, structured observation framework
